My vision: Breaking In

An initiative to demystify product management for everyone at ISB who doesn’t come from a PM or engineering background — and show them that’s actually an advantage, not a gap. I want BTC to be the place that builds that bridge.

Why I’m the right person to run this

I’m not a PM or an engineer — and that’s the point. I’ll notice the things that those with technical backgrounds might take for granted: the jargon that alienates people, the assumed context that never gets explained, the unwritten rules that make the path look harder than it is. My goal is to make those visible, and then dismantle them. Someone who’s been on the outside and found their way in is often better placed to build the door for others.

Page 02 — Ideas

Three things I’d build
as Events Coordinator

Each one solves a real BTC problem — and each one leverages technology to do it faster and smarter than a spreadsheet ever could.

01

BTC Member Portal

Problem: event sign-ups are scattered, feedback goes nowhere, and members have no single place to stay plugged in.

  • A dedicated BTC events hub — members can browse upcoming sessions, register in one click, and add events directly to their calendar (Outlook or Google).
  • Post-event, a structured feedback form is automatically surfaced. Responses feed into a live dashboard visible to the full committee.
  • Built on the same model as this site — lightweight, no-install, works on any device. I’ve already built a version of this for WIB.
🤖 AI-summarised feedback after every event
02

BTC Knowledge Hub

Problem: every cohort starts from zero. Speaker contacts, venue notes, what worked — gone at handoff.

  • A searchable repository of all past BTC events — recaps, feedback themes, speaker details, venue notes, and cross-club commitments.
  • AI auto-tags and summarises entries after each event so the log stays current without extra admin effort.
  • Any future EC can ask “what did we do for the last fintech session?” and get an actual answer — not a blank Drive folder.
🤖 AI-powered tagging & summarisation
03

Cross-Club Unified Calendar

Problem: clubs plan in silos. Clashes happen. High-demand weeks get overloaded. Students miss events they’d have attended.

  • A shared calendar aggregating BTC sessions alongside major club events, exam weeks, and CAPD touchpoints.
  • AI flags scheduling conflicts before they’re locked in and suggests better windows based on historical attendance data.
  • Shareable and visible to the full cohort — doubles as a marketing asset showing BTC’s presence across the year.
🤖 AI conflict detection & scheduling suggestions
