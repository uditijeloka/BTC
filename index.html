What I'd actually bring

Beyond running events well — I want to use this role to build something that didn't exist when I was figuring out my own path into product.

Breaking In

An initiative to demystify product management for everyone at ISB who doesn't come from a PM or engineering background — and show them that's actually an advantage, not a gap.

Why I'm the right person to run this

I'm not a PM. I'm not an engineer. That's exactly the point. I'll notice the things that PMs and engineers take for granted — the jargon that alienates people, the assumed context that never gets explained, the "unwritten rules" that make the path look harder than it is. My job will be to make those visible, and then dismantle them. BTC doesn't need another PM to talk about PM. It needs someone who's been on the outside — and can build a door.

01

BTC Knowledge Hub

Problem: every cohort starts from zero. Lessons learned, speaker contacts, what worked — gone at handoff.

  • A searchable repository of all past BTC events — recaps, feedback themes, speaker notes, venue details, and cross-club commitments.
  • AI auto-tags and summarises entries after each event so the log stays current without extra admin effort.
  • Any future EC can ask "what did we do for the last fintech session?" and get an actual answer — not a blank Drive folder.

02

Post-Event Intelligence Report

Problem: feedback forms get filled. Nobody reads them systematically. The next event is planned the same way.

  • After each event, drop in feedback responses and attendance data — AI generates a structured debrief in minutes.
  • Covers what landed, what didn't, sentiment by category (content, logistics, speaker, timing), and a clear "do differently" section.
  • Report goes to the full BTC committee — not just the EC — so improvements are visible and collective.
🤖 AI-generated debrief after every event
03

Cross-Club Unified Calendar

Problem: clubs plan in silos. Clashes happen. High-demand weeks get overloaded. Students miss things they'd have attended.

  • A shared calendar aggregating BTC sessions alongside major club events, exam weeks, and CAPD touchpoints.
  • AI flags scheduling conflicts before they're locked in and suggests better windows based on historical attendance data.
  • Shareable and visible to the full cohort — doubles as a marketing asset for BTC's activity across the year.
